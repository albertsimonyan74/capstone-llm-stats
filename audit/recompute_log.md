## Scope and rationale

- **Why reweight?** Equal weights treat numerical correctness as
  co-equal with reasoning + assumption articulation. The literature
  consistently identifies assumption violations and reasoning gaps as the
  load-bearing failure modes (Du 2025, Boye & Moell 2025, Yamauchi 2025);
  numerical correctness is necessary but trivially checkable
  (Liu 2025, Boye & Moell 2025).
- **What was recomputed?** Per-run aggregate scores, accuracy bootstrap
  CIs, robustness deltas (Layer 1 aggregate + Layer 2 per-dimension),
  per-dim calibration ECE, and the four poster figures
  (`three_rankings.png`, `a2_accuracy_by_category.png`,
  `a3_failure_heatmap.png`, `a6_aggregate_ranking.png`) plus two new
  figures (`a4b_per_dim_robustness.png`, `a5b_per_dim_calibration.png`).
- **What was NOT modified?** The two canonical aggregation paths
  (`evaluation/metrics.py`, `llm_runner/response_parser.py`) remain at
  equal 0.20 weights ŌĆö preserves v1 reproducibility. The new weights
  are applied only by `scripts/recompute_nmacr.py` to produce
  `experiments/results_v2/nmacr_scores_v2.jsonl`, which is now the
  single source of truth for the literature-weighted aggregate.

% --- Appendix A: Formal Metric Definitions ---

\label{app:metrics}

This appendix collects formal definitions for the five quantitative
metrics in the main text and the bootstrap procedure used for all
reported confidence intervals.

\subsection{NMACR Aggregate}

See Eq.~(\ref{eq:nmacr}) in \S\ref{sec:scoring} for the NMACR
aggregate definition. The weight ordering $A > R > M > C > N$
reflects assumption articulation as the foundation of Bayesian
reasoning; a correct numeric answer without derivation does not
support the answer logic. The full literature trail behind the
weight values lives in the scoring metadata.

\subsection{Robustness Score Ratio}

See Eq.~(\ref{eq:robustness}). The pool is $n=398$ paired (base,
perturbation) records, a subset of the 473-record superset after
dropping rows with missing or errored runs.

\subsection{Expected Calibration Error}

See Eq.~(\ref{eq:ece}); $B = 4$ confidence buckets are parsed from
hedging language (low, medium, high, unstated), with bucket centres
$0.25$, $0.50$, $0.85$, $0.50$ for computing $\mathrm{conf}(B_b)$.

\subsection{Krippendorff's \texorpdfstring{$\alpha$}{alpha}}

Inter-rater reliability between keyword rubric and judge is
$\alpha = 1 - D_o / D_e$ ($D_o$ observed, $D_e$ chance-expected
disagreement), computed on the three Path~B dimensions.

\subsection{Spearman Rank Correlation}

Per-dimension agreement is also reported as
$\rho = 1 - 6 \sum d_i^2 / [n(n^2 - 1)]$ with $d_i$ the rank
difference; $\rho$ is preferred over Pearson $r$ because the
rubric-judge relationship is monotonic but not linear.

\subsection{Bootstrap Procedure}

All CIs use the 95\% percentile bootstrap on per-run aggregates,
with $B = 10{,}000$ resamples (seed 42). Resamples preserve
per-model count ($n=171$ accuracy, $n=398$ robustness).
